Task

Each video in the ISSA archive contains one or more recorded TV programmes separated by visual markers (countdowns, colour bars, black screens, title cards). The model is given a video and must return the start and end timecode of every programme. Predicted segments are matched to ground-truth segments by maximum temporal overlap; the comparison score measures what fraction of each ground-truth segment is covered by its best-matching prediction, averaged across all segments (0 = no coverage, 1 = perfect coverage).

Observations

2B model — inadequate; consistently scores near 0 across all test videos.
4B (full precision) — reasoning tokens consume >5k tokens, overrunning the 32k context on long videos.
4B-AWQ-4bit — preliminary results only; aobbu34200001 runs returned no valid output.
9B-AWQ-4bit — best overall; fits on 24 GB VRAM (~23 GB); no-reasoning mode recommended.
DVC43313 — hardest video (only 2 ground-truth programmes); models tend to over-segment significantly.
Seed variance — notable run-to-run variability (e.g. 9B: 0.99 vs 0.16 on DVC43998); multiple seeds needed.
VRAM — all models run within 24 GB when quantised; 27B-GPTQ-Int4 does not fit.
Next steps — 9B & 27B on HPC, reasoning on/off comparison, KV-cache FP8 calibration.
